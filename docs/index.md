## FROGS

Frogs are fascinating amphibians with unique adaptations, like breathing through their skin, swallowing with their eyes, and many species can jump 20 times their own height. 
Here are some more fun facts about frogs:
- They are cold-blooded: Frogs, like other amphibians, are ectothermic, meaning their body temperature fluctuates with the environment. 
- They have three ways to breathe: Frogs can breathe through their skin, lungs, and even through the lining of their mouth. 
- They have teeth: Most frogs have teeth, but only on their upper jaw, which they use to help hold onto prey. 
- They can freeze solid: Certain species of frogs, like the wood frog, can freeze nearly solid in the winter and emerge unscathed in the spring. 
- They shed their skin: Frogs regularly shed their skin, and they often eat the shed skin.
